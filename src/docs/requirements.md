## Functional Requirements

1. The system shall allow users to create an account and log in.
2. The system shall allow class representatives to mark attendance for their classes.
3. The system shall allow lecturers to view attendance records for their courses.
4. The system shall allow students to view their own attendance records.
5. The system shall automatically compute attendance percentages for students.
6. The system shall provide real-time access to attendance data for all users.
7. The system shall allow administrators to manage user accounts and oversee system operations.
8. The system shall integrate attendance data with existing academic systems for comprehensive data management and reporting.
9. The system shall send notifications to students and lecturers regarding attendance status.
10. The system shall provide a user-friendly interface for all users.
11. The system shall implement robust security measures to protect sensitive attendance data and ensure user privacy.
12. The system shall reduce administrative costs associated with manual attendance management and minimize the need for physical storage of records.
13. The system shall offer insights into attendance patterns and trends through data analytics.
14. The system shall allow users to generate customized reports based on specific criteria.
15. The system shall provide a mobile-friendly interface for on-the-go access to attendance records and management.
16. The system shall support multiple languages to cater to the diverse linguistic needs of the user base.
17. The system shall support integration with biometric attendance systems for enhanced security and accuracy in attendance recording.
18. The system shall utilize cloud-based storage solutions for secure and scalable storage of attendance data.
19. The system shall implement automatic backup and recovery mechanisms to safeguard attendance data against loss or corruption.
20. The system shall implement role-based access control to ensure that users only have access to the features and data relevant to their roles.
21. The system shall maintain a comprehensive audit trail of all attendance-related activities for transparency and accountability.
22. The system shall allow institutions to define and customize attendance policies based on their specific requirements.
23. The system shall provide a secure and efficient digital attendance system to eliminate paper-based attendance recording and ensure accurate tracking and automatic computation of attendance percentages.
24. The system shall improve transparency and accountability in academic participation by providing real-time access to attendance data for all users.
25. The system shall enforce institutional attendance policies through automation, ensuring consistent application across the institution.
26. The system shall facilitate long-term storage and easy retrieval of attendance records for institutional use.
27. The system shall reduce paper waste and promote environmentally friendly practices within the institution.
28. The system shall be designed to accommodate the growing needs of the institution as it expands its student body and course offerings.
29. The system shall provide an intuitive and easy-to-use interface for all users, enhancing user experience and adoption rates.
30. The system shall implement robust security measures to protect sensitive attendance data and ensure user privacy, including encryption and secure authentication mechanisms.
31. The system shall reduce administrative costs associated with manual attendance management and minimize the need for physical storage of records by digitalizing the attendance management process.
32. The system shall send timely notifications to students and lecturers regarding attendance status, ensuring proactive communication and engagement.
33. The system shall offer insights into attendance patterns and trends through data analytics, enabling informed decision-making for academic planning and interventions.
34. The system shall allow users to generate customized reports based on specific criteria, facilitating targeted analysis and reporting for academic and administrative purposes.

## Non-Functional Requirements

1. The system shall be available 99.9% of the time to ensure continuous access for users.
2. The system shall respond to user actions within 2 seconds to ensure a smooth user experience.
3. The system shall be scalable to accommodate up to 10,000 concurrent users without performance degradation.
4. The system shall be compatible with major web browsers (e.g., Chrome, Firefox, Safari) and mobile devices (iOS and Android) to ensure accessibility for all users.
5. The system shall implement data encryption for sensitive information to ensure data security and privacy.
6. The system shall comply with relevant data protection regulations (e.g., GDPR) to ensure legal compliance and protect user privacy.
7. The system shall have a user-friendly interface that is intuitive and easy to navigate for all users, regardless of their technical proficiency.
8. The system shall provide regular updates and maintenance to ensure optimal performance and security.
9. The system shall have a backup and disaster recovery plan in place to ensure data integrity and continuity of operations in case of system failure or data loss.
10. The system shall have a comprehensive logging and monitoring mechanism to track system performance, user activity, and potential security incidents for proactive management and troubleshooting.
11. The system shall be designed with accessibility in mind, ensuring that it is usable by individuals with disabilities, including those who rely on assistive technologies.
12. The system shall be developed using a modular architecture to facilitate maintenance, scalability, and future enhancements.
13. The system shall have a clear and comprehensive documentation to assist users in understanding and utilizing the system effectively, as well as to guide developers in maintaining and enhancing the system over time.
14. The system shall be designed to minimize downtime during updates and maintenance, ensuring that users can continue to access the system with minimal disruption.
15. The system shall have a robust authentication and authorization mechanism to ensure that only authorized users can access the system and perform actions based on their roles and permissions.
16. The system shall be designed to handle high volumes of data and user interactions without compromising performance or user experience, ensuring that it can scale effectively as the institution grows and user demand increases.
17. The system shall be designed to be easily maintainable and extensible, allowing for future enhancements and new features to be added without significant disruption to existing functionality or user experience.
18. The system shall be designed to be resilient to common security threats, such as SQL injection, cross-site scripting (XSS), and cross-site request forgery (CSRF), to ensure the security and integrity of the system and its data.
19. The system shall be designed to provide a seamless and consistent user experience across different devices and platforms, ensuring that users can access and use the system effectively regardless of their preferred device or platform.
20. The system shall be designed to support integration with third-party services and APIs, allowing for enhanced functionality and interoperability with other systems used by the institution, such as learning management systems (LMS) and student information systems (SIS).
